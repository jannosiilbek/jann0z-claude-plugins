## Captured content

### Definition
"A persona is a fictional, yet realistic, description of a typical or target user of the
product. Personas help product teams empathize with users."

### Why personas are used (benefits)
- **Memorability:** humans connect more strongly with "concrete instances than
  generalizations" — a single named character beats an abstract statistic.
- **Empathy:** focusing on one individual builds genuine understanding of needs.
- **Shared vocabulary:** the persona's name becomes team shorthand for a cluster of
  attributes, desires, and behaviors.
- **Ongoing utility:** beyond design, personas guide expert reviews, usability-study
  recruiting, and analytics segmentation.

### How to create them
**Research phase:** ground personas in real user research (field studies, surveys,
interviews, longitudinal studies); involve the team to increase buy-in/adoption.
**Creation phase:** identify and cluster user characteristics from research → merge similar
groups and drop less-relevant ones → add specific, purpose-driven details for believability
and recall.

### What to include
Name, age, gender, photo; a tagline summarizing the character; experience level with the
product; the context of interaction; goals and concerns during relevant tasks; quotes
reflecting attitude.

### What to exclude
Anything not tied to a design decision. "Each piece of information should have a purpose for
being included." Irrelevant details obscure essentials and reduce memorability.
