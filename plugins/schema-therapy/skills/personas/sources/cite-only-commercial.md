## 1. About Face: The Essentials of Interaction Design, 4th Edition  — CANONICAL

- **Authors:** Alan Cooper, Robert Reimann, David Cronin, Christopher Noessel
  (with Jason Csizmadi, Doug LeMoine credited on the cover/contributors).
- **Edition:** 4th (current latest — confirmed no 5th edition exists as of 2026-06-04).
- **Publisher:** John Wiley & Sons (Wiley).
- **Year:** 2014.
- **ISBN-13:** 9781118766576 (print); 9781118766408 (e-book, per VitalSource).
- **Publisher page:** https://www.wiley.com/en-gb/About+Face:+The+Essentials+of+Interaction+Design,+4th+Edition-p-9781118766576
  (returns HTTP 403 to automated fetch; identity corroborated via Wiley listing, VitalSource,
  Amazon 1118766571, ACM Digital Library DOI 10.5555/2688796, Google Books id w9Q5BAAAQBAJ.)

**Why canonical (authority #1 for this skill):** This is the definitive systematic statement
of goal-directed personas. It is the home of:
- The **goal taxonomy** the skill depends on — **experience goals** (how the user wants to
  feel; visceral), **end goals** (what the user wants to accomplish; behavioral), and **life
  goals** (who the user wants to be; reflective). Successful products satisfy all three
  levels.
- The **persona-construction method** (clustering behavioral variables from research into
  patterns; the persona as a single composite portrait with name, photo, role, goals, quote).
- **Persona types / roles in design:** primary, secondary, supplemental, customer, served,
  and negative personas; selection of the **primary persona** that the design's primary
  interface is targeted at.

**Public-excerpt pointers (legitimately free, for verification only — do not store as text):**
- Ch. 5 "Modeling Users: Personas and Goals" course PDF: http://www.paulos.net/teaching/2011/BID/readings/AboutFaceCh5.pdf
  (an earlier-edition chapter; goal taxonomy substantially unchanged in the 4th ed.)
- "Users, Personas and Goals" CMU reading PDF: https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~jhm/Readings/cooper_personas.pdf

## 3. Know Your Customers' "Jobs to Be Done" — Christensen et al. (JTBD canon)

- **Authors:** Clayton M. Christensen, Taddy Hall, Karen Dillon, David S. Duncan.
- **Publication:** *Harvard Business Review*, September 2016 issue (feature article).
- **URL (paywalled):** https://hbr.org/2016/09/know-your-customers-jobs-to-be-done
  (video companion: https://hbr.org/video/5852531897001/know-your-customers-jobs-to-be-done)
- **Class:** cite-only (commercial / paywalled HBR).

**ADMISSION RULING — ADMITTED AS SUBORDINATE / COMPLEMENTARY (not authoritative).**
Honest assessment per the mandate: JTBD is a *complementary* lens, not the spine of this
skill. It is admitted because the skill's output is explicitly "goal-directed personas
**with jobs-to-be-done**," and Christensen's 2016 HBR article is the canonical, citable
statement of JTBD (the "milkshake" / "people hire products to do a job" framing). It is
**subordinate to Cooper**: where the two overlap, Cooper's goal-directed model governs the
*persona* (the goal-differentiated archetype); JTBD contributes the *job* framing (the
circumstance-driven progress a persona is trying to make). JTBD must never displace the
persona as the unit of modeling — downstream skills (08 task model per persona-job, 09
UI-flow per persona) key off the **persona**, with the **job** as the persona's goal in
context. Christensen's own canonical earlier origin is "The Cause and the Cure of Marketing
Malpractice" (HBR, 2005) and the milkshake case — cited for lineage only.
