sibling files linked below. Validation date for every row: **2026-06-04**.

## Authority alignment (who rules on overlapping concepts)

- **Definitions of goal / actor / impact / deliverable and the four-level hierarchy** →
  authoritative source is the **book (cite-only)**, with [`impactmapping-org-drawing.md`](./impactmapping-org-drawing.md)
  as the openly-licensed verbatim statement of the same definitions. The skill should treat `drawing.md` as the
  operational definition of record; the book is the deeper citation behind it.
- **The Why/Who/How/What question mapping** → [`impactmapping-org-home.md`](./impactmapping-org-home.md) and
  `drawing.md` agree exactly; use either. (`Why`=goal, `Who`=actors, `How`=impacts, `What`=deliverables.)
- **"Impacts are behaviour changes, NOT deliverables/features" (the central discipline)** →
  ruled by `drawing.md` + `about.md` (Defining quality section: a technically-correct deliverable that
  supports no impact "is a failure"). The book's "Typical Mapping Mistakes" chapter is the deeper cite-only
  authority on the deliverables-as-impacts anti-pattern.
- **SMART goals discipline** → the book is authoritative (Part III preparation steps "discover the real goal,
  define measurements"). The openly-licensed pages assert measurability ("capture metrics for key impacts")
  but do not spell out the SMART acronym; cite the book for the SMART framing.
- **When/how to run a session (modes, who participates)** → [`impactmapping-org-facilitation.md`](./impactmapping-org-facilitation.md)
  is authoritative and openly licensed.
- **Context selection (iterate / align / experiment / discover)** → the **InfoQ article (cite-only)** by
  Domingues, Berndtsson & Adzic is authoritative; it post-dates and refines the book.
- **Method lineage / origin** → the book's credit section (cite-only) is the primary authority; the IASDR 2019
  paper (cite-only, scholarly) corroborates the effect-mapping/inUse/Brinkerhoff lineage independently.
- **No contradictions found** across admitted sources. The openly-licensed site is a faithful, narrower
  restatement of the book; the InfoQ article extends rather than conflicts.

## Exclusions (every rejection justified)

- **Pirated full-text PDFs/EPUBs** (oceanofpdf.com, epage.pub, and similar) — copyright-infringing copies of
  the commercial book. Rejected on legality; the book is cite-only.
- **Internet Archive borrowable scan** (archive.org/details/impactmappingmak0000gojk) — a controlled-digital-
  lending scan of the copyrighted book; not openly licensed. Rejected as a content source (the book is
  cite-only); listed here only to note it exists.
- **Retailer/aggregator listings** (Amazon, Goodreads, Google Books, Apple Books, Booktopia, Waterstones,
  AbeBooks, Thriftbooks, Book Depository, eBay) — used only to confirm bibliographic identity/edition; carry
  no authoritative methodology content of their own. Not admitted as content sources.
- **Third-party explainer blogs/tutorials** (agilepainrelief.com, amplitude.com, selleo.com, desmart.com,
  techtarget.com, productplan.com, productfolio.com, growingscrummasters.com, medium.com posts, marcabraham.com,
  ebgconsulting review, insideproduct.co, plays-in-business.com, etc.) — secondary restatements/reviews of
  Adzic's method. Rejected to keep admission conservative: they add no authority over the primary sources and
  risk importing their authors' drift (e.g., loose impact-vs-deliverable usage). Several are usefully indexed
  inside `impactmapping-org-tips-and-tricks-index.md` for discovery, but are not gathered as authorities.
- **Tool/template vendors** (Miro, Creately, UXPressia, EdrawMind, Draft.io, Wondershare) — generic diagramming
  canvases. They host impact-map templates but are not authoritative statements of the formalism, and are not
  "executable" artifacts of it. Rejected.
- **IIBA blog "Achieving Goals With the SMART Concept and Impact Mapping"** — a third-party BA-community
  article. The SMART discipline it discusses is better cited to the book; rejected as redundant secondary.
- **inUse "The many faces of Impact Mapping"** — vendor/origin-agency marketing page; lineage facts it covers
  are already carried authoritatively by the book's credit section and the peer-reviewed IASDR paper. Rejected
  as redundant.

### Executable materials — verify-none (expected, confirmed)

Impact Mapping is a **facilitation and diagramming formalism** (a collaboratively-grown mind map). It has **no
executable / runnable artifact** — no schema, grammar, linter, CLI, or reference implementation. The official
site ships only prose, illustrations, and (in the workshop repo) facilitation exercises and slide decks; the
royalty-free "consultants" artwork is diagram imagery, not code. Searches for an executable/tool/plugin/
generator surfaced only third-party drawing apps (excluded above). **No executable source exists or is
expected; the executable class is correctly empty.**
