## Official framing (from eventstorming.com/book)

"Introducing EventStorming" by Alberto Brandolini is the official book and the recognized
canonical text. The site openly states the book is still under active writing, with FIXMEs
and incomplete chapters: Big Picture mechanics are largely covered, while Process Design
and Software Design are far from complete.

## The three established formats (recognized canon)

EventStorming is a family of workshop formats sharing the same base (orange domain events
on a timeline). The three canonical levels, in increasing precision:

1. **Big Picture EventStorming** — explore/assess a whole line of business or domain;
   10-30+ people; output feeds Conway's-law alignment and emerging bounded contexts.
   (Online delivery is explicitly NOT recommended for Big Picture.)
2. **Process Modelling EventStorming** — model an existing or designed process; introduces
   commands, read models, and policies. Miro template exists.
3. **Software Design EventStorming** — design-level; produces a model close to code;
   introduces aggregates/constraints and bounded contexts. Miro template exists. Does not
   fit a 90-minute timebox.

## Notation philosophy (the "pizza" rule)

The notation is open and extensible: orange events + the timeline are the mandatory base
("the dough"); other stickies (commands, policies, read models, actors, systems, aggregates,
hotspots) are added "as toppings" as the conversation needs them. This is the canonical
"picture that explains (almost) everything" stance — a fixed core grammar with optional,
purpose-driven extensions.
